Key Projects

CR25 Rear Wing Design

Increased the efficiency of the rear wing by 11% and reduced its weight by nearly 1.5 lbs. This was achieved through iterative design using CFD and SolidWorks to reduce pressure gradients and minimize vortices off the endplate. The result was lower induced drag and increased aerodynamic efficiency.

CR22 Side Aero Design

Created the first iterations of side aero for Crimson Racing. This introduced me to CFD and SolidWorks, and taught me how to use them in iterative design. The work laid the foundation for future side aero development.

Glider Project

In my freshman year, I designed and built a model glider as part of my Intro to Engineering class. The glider successfully carried 24 quarters a distance of 40.3 feet.

This project marked my first experience with a real engineering challenge — and when I knew I wanted to become an engineer.

The wing construction was a highlight: I imported an airfoil from Airfoil Tools into SolidWorks, added a spar slot, 3D printed the ribs, mounted them to a balsa wood spar, and used a lightweight paper skin for covering.

Python Dive Calculator

For my final project in an Intro to Python course, I built a scuba dive calculator. The program takes various inputs such as tank pressure and desired depth, and calculates how long you can explore safely while ensuring adequate air for surfacing.

Flag Deposit Box (Eagle Scout Project)

I planned, acquired donated materials, and led my troop in building a flag deposit box. I managed task delegation and secured approval from the church committee for its placement.

The box is still in use today and averages 30 deposited flags per month, all of which are properly retired.
